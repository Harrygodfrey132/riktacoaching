  <section class="guide-section" id="what-is-autism">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Basics</span>
        <h2>What is autism?</h2>
        <p>
          Autism describes a way of functioning that affects social interaction, communication, and flexibility.
          The spectrum means two people can have the same diagnosis but very different strengths, interests, and support needs.
        </p>
        <p>
          In the 1940s Leo Kanner and Hans Asperger described similar patterns in children.
          Explanations that blamed parents have long been dismissed – we now know causes are complex and include genetic, biological, and environmental factors.
        </p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="guide-section" id="symptoms">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Symptoms</span>
        <h2>How can autism show up?</h2>
        <p>Each person has a unique profile of strengths and challenges. Many also have clear strengths in detail focus, concentration, and honesty.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="guide-card-grid">
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Social &amp; communication</h3>
          <p>Challenges with reciprocity, eye contact, facial expression, and reading irony or subtext.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Patterns &amp; interests</h3>
          <p>Repetitive behaviors, need for routines, and intense interests that create focus but also inflexibility.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Sensory</h3>
          <p>Sensitivity to sound, light, touch, or smells. Overload can lead to fatigue or shutdown.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Communication styles</h3>
          <p>Some communicate mainly in writing or with support tools. Responses can take time – clarity and patience help.</p>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="guide-section" id="diagnostic-criteria">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Diagnostics</span>
        <h2>Diagnostic criteria (DSM-5 – in brief)</h2>
        <p>Two criteria must be met – and impact daily life across settings.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="guide-card-grid">
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>1. Social communication</h3>
          <p>Persistent difficulties with reciprocity, communication, and relationships.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>2. Restricted &amp; repetitive behaviors</h3>
          <p>At least several aspects such as stereotypies, need for routines, special interests, or sensory differences.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Support needs</h3>
          <p>The diagnosis also notes support level, co-occurring conditions (e.g., ADHD), and cognitive profile.</p>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="guide-section" id="how-autism-shows">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Variations</span>
        <h2>How does autism show?</h2>
        <p>Visibility is shaped by age, demands, and strategies.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="guide-card-grid">
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Degree &amp; age</h3>
          <p>Most apparent when demands rise (preschool, school, new environments). Adults may have developed working strategies.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Gender &amp; masking</h3>
          <p>Many girls/women mask by copying and over-performing – a strategy that costs energy and can lead to burnout.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Context-dependent</h3>
          <p>Difficulties show where demands are highest – socially, with changes, or when special interests must be interrupted.</p>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="guide-section" id="assessment">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Assessment</span>
        <h2>Autism assessment – how it works</h2>
        <p>Public and private assessments follow the same building blocks.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="guide-card-grid">
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Medical review</h3>
          <p>A physician maps health and rules out other causes.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Psychological assessment</h3>
          <p>Structured interviews on strengths, challenges, and timeline from childhood.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Family input</h3>
          <p>Parents or partner add context to capture everyday life.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Forms &amp; tests</h3>
          <p>Self-ratings and neuropsychological tests are weighed in.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Feedback</h3>
          <p>Clear results, diagnosis if criteria are met, and recommendations for study, work, and daily life.</p>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="guide-section" id="parent-perspective">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Parents</span>
        <h2>Being a parent to a child with autism</h2>
        <p>Pride in your child’s interests can coexist with worries about school, friendships, and the future.</p>
      </div>
      <div>
        <ul class="guide-list">
          <li><strong>Education:</strong> parent programs and habilitation provide tools.</li>
          <li><strong>Support groups:</strong> in-person or online forums reduce isolation.</li>
          <li><strong>Visual routines:</strong> schedules, timers, and preparation create safety.</li>
          <li><strong>Involvement:</strong> plan together with your child to strengthen control and confidence.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="guide-section" id="everyday-strategies">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Daily strategies</span>
        <h2>What can you do yourself?</h2>
        <p>Small, consistent adaptations make a big difference day to day.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="guide-card-grid">
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Create structure</h3>
          <p>Daily routines, visual schedules, and clear answers to what, when, how long, and what happens next.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Sensory environment</h3>
          <p>Dim sound and light, create a calm space, and use aids (ear protection, tactile objects) when needed.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Support interests</h3>
          <p>Let special interests drive learning and motivation.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Clear communication</h3>
          <p>Be concrete, avoid ambiguity that causes stress, and respect alternative communication methods.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>School &amp; work</h3>
          <p>Clear instructions, reduced distractions, room for breaks, and flexibility in tasks.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Social support &amp; independence</h3>
          <p>Social skills training, help expressing needs, and stepwise plans for independence.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Professional support</h3>
          <p>Behavior therapy, occupational therapy, psychoeducation, and family support ease daily life for everyone involved.</p>
        </article>
      </div>
