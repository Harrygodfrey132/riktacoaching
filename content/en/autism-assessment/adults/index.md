<section class="who-section adhd-definition" id="vad-ar-adhd">
  <div class="container">
    <div class="who-grid intro-grid">
      <div class="intro-copy">
        <h2>What does autism mean in adulthood?</h2>
        <p>
          Autism is a neurodevelopmental profile that affects how we read social situations, process sensory input,
          and organise daily life. As an adult it can show up as fatigue after social events, focused special interests,
          or a strong need for predictability.
        </p>
        <p>
          During the assessment we focus on your life history, masking strategies, and what gives you recovery. We separate
          autism from stress, ADHD, or other co-occurring conditions to give a conclusion that holds over time.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
          “A good autism assessment is about understanding both strengths and potential barriers. It gives language for your
          experience and opens the door to the adaptations you need.”
        </blockquote>
      </div>
      <div class="intro-stats adhd-definition-card">
        <h3>During the assessment we look at:</h3>
        <ul>
          <li>Social communication, masking, and energy balance after different situations.</li>
          <li>Sensory sensitivity, need for structure, and recovery strategies.</li>
          <li>Life course perspective – school, relationships, work, and special interests.</li>
          <li>Co-occurring conditions such as ADHD, anxiety, depression, or EDS that affect the whole picture.</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">
          We use tools like RAADS-14, AQ, 3Di, and SRS-2 together with clinical interviews and input from relatives when desired.
        </p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="who-section adhd-process adhd-process--timeline" id="process">
  <div class="container">
    <div class="section-intro">
      <span class="eyebrow">Autism assessment for adults</span>
      <h2>A careful process that brings clarity</h2>
      <p class="muted">
        Our autism assessment for adults is a structured, evidence-based process to see whether you are on the spectrum and what
        support you need. The team includes experienced psychologists and psychiatrists who follow national guidelines and make
        sure you feel safe and understood at every step.
      </p>
      <p class="muted">
        Many come to us after long-standing challenges in social situations or daily life, sometimes after a relative received a
        diagnosis or recognising earlier struggles. Whatever your situation, the goal is to understand your strengths and
        difficulties better and get the right help going forward.
      </p>
    </div>
    <div class="timeline">
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>1</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Initial assessment call</h3>
          <p>
            We start with a 30-minute assessment call where you explain your concerns and why you seek an evaluation.
            We review current difficulties, what you hope to gain, and decide whether a full neuropsychiatric assessment
            is indicated. If yes, we plan the next steps right away; otherwise you receive advice and referrals.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>2</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>In-depth interviews and questionnaires</h3>
          <p>
            A licensed psychologist conducts a detailed history from childhood to today focusing on social interaction,
            communication, routines, and sensory sensitivity. When possible we also interview a relative who has known you
            since childhood. You complete standardised self-assessments that map autistic traits and related symptoms.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>3</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Psychological testing and medical review</h3>
          <p>
            You complete psychological tests of cognitive and social functions. In parallel a specialist physician or psychiatrist
            performs a medical and psychiatric review to rule out other causes and identify co-occurring conditions such as ADHD,
            depression, or anxiety.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>4</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Multidisciplinary review</h3>
          <p>
            Psychologist and psychiatrist review all information together and determine whether criteria for autism are met and
            whether other diagnoses are present. The aim is a correct, evidence-based diagnosis and a clear overall picture.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>5</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Feedback and follow-up</h3>
          <p>
            We schedule a feedback session to go through results and conclusions verbally, and you receive a written report.
            You get tailored recommendations for everyday life, work, psychoeducation, treatment, or further referrals. Even if
            a diagnosis is not set you receive clear feedback and guidance on next steps.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
