<section class="coachning-section" id="entrepreneur-adhd-friction">
  <div class="coachning-panel">
    <h2>Why ADHD can be both an advantage and a problem in entrepreneurship</h2>
    <p>
      Running your own business often rewards speed, initiative, intuition, and resilience. Those can be real ADHD strengths. The problem is that self-employment also requires sustained follow-through, decisions without external deadlines, admin you cannot ignore, and the ability to keep showing up when the work stops being novel. That is where many entrepreneurs start losing time, money, and confidence.
    </p>
    <ul class="coachning-list">
      <li><strong>Idea overload:</strong> You generate too many possible directions and lose traction on the one that matters now.</li>
      <li><strong>Revenue drift:</strong> Important commercial work gets crowded out by lower-stakes but easier tasks.</li>
      <li><strong>Admin backlog:</strong> Invoices, email, proposals, bookkeeping, and follow-up linger until they become stressful.</li>
      <li><strong>Inconsistent execution:</strong> You can work intensely in bursts, then struggle to maintain steady progress.</li>
      <li><strong>Blurred boundaries:</strong> Work spills into all hours, but still does not always move in the right direction.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="coachning-section" id="entrepreneur-coaching-focus">
  <div class="coachning-panel">
    <h2>What ADHD coaching for entrepreneurs actually helps with</h2>
    <p>
      Entrepreneur coaching focuses on the execution problems that show up when you are responsible for everything. The aim is not to flatten your strengths. It is to stop your business being held back by avoidable friction, scattered attention, and a lack of structure around the work that matters most.
    </p>
    <ul class="coachning-list">
      <li><strong>Prioritising revenue work:</strong> Keep client delivery, sales, outreach, or product progress ahead of lower-value tasks.</li>
      <li><strong>Planning the week:</strong> Build a structure that tells you what matters now instead of relying on mood or urgency.</li>
      <li><strong>Decision-making:</strong> Reduce time lost to overthinking, endless options, or changing direction too quickly.</li>
      <li><strong>Admin and operations:</strong> Create workable systems for invoices, email, follow-up, and routine business maintenance.</li>
      <li><strong>Finishing and shipping:</strong> Improve completion so good ideas become delivered work, launched offers, or closed loops.</li>
      <li><strong>Boundaries and pacing:</strong> Stop the business from taking every hour while still feeling disorganised.</li>
      <li><strong>Burnout prevention:</strong> Build a more sustainable rhythm for ambition, execution, and recovery.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</section>
