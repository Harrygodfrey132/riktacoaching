<section class="coachning-section" id="why-students-struggle">
  <div class="coachning-panel">
    <h2>Why ADHD can hit hard in education</h2>
    <p>
      Student life demands self-management all day long. You are expected to plan ahead, organise your week, track deadlines, switch between classes, revise independently, remember admin, and still look like you are coping. ADHD often makes those hidden demands far harder than they appear from the outside.
    </p>
    <ul class="coachning-list">
      <li><strong>Deadline blindness:</strong> Work can feel far away until it becomes urgent all at once.</li>
      <li><strong>Task initiation problems:</strong> Starting coursework can feel disproportionately hard, even when you care about it.</li>
      <li><strong>Inconsistent focus:</strong> You may be able to focus intensely sometimes, then struggle badly on ordinary tasks.</li>
      <li><strong>Overload and shutdown:</strong> When too many demands build up, avoidance can start to look like laziness from the outside.</li>
      <li><strong>Emotional knock-on effects:</strong> Repeated missed deadlines and last-minute panic can damage confidence fast.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="coachning-section" id="what-coaching-covers">
  <div class="coachning-panel">
    <h2>What ADHD coaching for students actually helps with</h2>
    <p>
      This is not generic study advice. Coaching is about finding the points where your week keeps breaking down and replacing them with systems that are simple enough to repeat under pressure.
    </p>
    <ul class="coachning-list">
      <li><strong>Planning coursework:</strong> Break essays, revision, and projects into smaller actions with clearer starting points.</li>
      <li><strong>Keeping up with deadlines:</strong> Build a weekly structure that surfaces what matters early enough to act on it.</li>
      <li><strong>Revision without overload:</strong> Create realistic study blocks instead of relying on marathon sessions.</li>
      <li><strong>Email, portals, and admin:</strong> Stop losing energy to forgotten forms, unread messages, and avoidable last-minute problems.</li>
      <li><strong>Attendance and transitions:</strong> Improve getting to class, switching tasks, and recovering after demanding days.</li>
      <li><strong>Emotional regulation:</strong> Reduce shame, panic, and shutdown patterns around work you have been avoiding.</li>
      <li><strong>Communication:</strong> Get better at asking for support, speaking to tutors, or handling group work and expectations.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</section>
