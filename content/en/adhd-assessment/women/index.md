        <h2>How can ADHD show up in women?</h2>
        <p>
          ADHD affects attention, impulse control, and motivation. In women, symptoms can be more internal — mental worry,
          perfectionistic demands, or recurring exhaustion after periods of high tempo.
        </p>
        <p>
          We explore how life stages, hormones, and masking strategies affect your everyday life. Together we distinguish ADHD
          from stress, anxiety, or PMDD and land on an explanation that fits your experience.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
          “When you have words for your patterns, it becomes easier to choose what to change and what support you need.”
        </blockquote>
      </div>
      <div class="intro-stats adhd-definition-card">
        <h3>During the assessment we look at:</h3>
        <ul>
          <li>Energy cycles over the month, seasons, and major life phases.</li>
          <li>Masking, performance patterns, and the impact of long-term stress.</li>
          <li>Emotion regulation, sleep, and co-occurring issues like anxiety or depression.</li>
          <li>Protective factors, relationships, and practical support in daily life.</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">

<section class="who-section adhd-process adhd-process--timeline" id="process">
  <div class="container">
    <div class="section-intro">
      <span class="eyebrow">Adult ADHD assessment</span>
      <h2>A process that brings clarity and the right support</h2>
      <p class="muted">
        Our adult ADHD assessment is designed to identify ADHD or related conditions in people 18 or older. Many seek help
        after long-standing difficulties with attention, organisation, restlessness, or impulsivity that affect work, studies,
        and relationships. The goal is clarity and the right treatment — for many it is a relief to understand the roots of
        their challenges.
      </p>
    </div>
    <div class="timeline">
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>1</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Screening call</h3>
          <p>
            We start with a 30-minute screening call. You share your concerns and why you want an assessment. We review your
            current difficulties and what you hope to gain. The call decides if a full neuropsychiatric assessment is needed.
            If yes, we plan next steps right away — if no, we offer advice and referrals.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>2</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>In-depth interviews and forms</h3>
          <p>
            A licensed psychologist gathers a detailed history from childhood to now — upbringing, school, work, relationships,
            and daily functioning — with focus on social interaction, attention, and hyperactivity. When possible we also
            interview someone who knew you in childhood. You complete standardised self-ratings that map ADHD traits and
            related symptoms.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>3</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Psychological testing and medical review</h3>
          <p>
            You complete psychological tests of cognitive and social functions. In parallel, a specialist doctor or
            psychiatrist performs a medical and psychiatric review to rule out other causes and identify co-occurring
            conditions such as autism, depression, or anxiety.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>4</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Multidisciplinary case review</h3>
          <p>
            Psychologist and psychiatrist review all information together and assess whether ADHD criteria are met and if other
            diagnoses are present. The aim is an accurate, evidence-based diagnosis.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>5</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Feedback and follow-up</h3>
          <p>
            We book a feedback session to go through results and conclusions verbally, and you receive a written report. You
            get tailored recommendations such as adjustments at home or work, psychoeducation, treatment, or referrals. Even
            without a diagnosis you receive clear feedback and guidance on next steps.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

        <summary>How do hormones affect the assessment?</summary>

          We map symptoms across the cycle and consider pregnancy, breastfeeding, or menopause. When needed, we collaborate
          with other professionals or providers.
        </p>
      </details>
      <details>
        <summary>I already have a burnout diagnosis — can I still get ADHD support?</summary>
        <p>
          Yes. Many women with ADHD have experienced burnout. We separate the symptoms and create a plan that respects recovery
          and sustainable routines.
        </p>
      </details>
      <details>
        <summary>Do I need to involve family or friends?</summary>
        <p>
          It’s optional. Some choose to invite a partner or friend for part of the process to add perspective, but it’s up to you.
        </p>
      </details>
      <details>
        <summary>What if the assessment shows something other than ADHD?</summary>
