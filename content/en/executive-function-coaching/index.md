<section class="coachning-section" id="what-executive-function-means">
  <div class="coachning-panel">
    <h2>What executive function coaching actually helps with</h2>
    <p>
      Executive function is the set of mental skills that helps you organise action. It is what allows you to break a task down, decide what comes first, remember the next step, start without too much delay, and keep going without everything drifting. When those systems are strained, life starts to feel more complicated than it should.
    </p>
    <ul class="coachning-list">
      <li><strong>Planning:</strong> Turn vague intentions into a structure you can actually follow.</li>
      <li><strong>Prioritising:</strong> Stop everything feeling equally urgent or equally avoidable.</li>
      <li><strong>Task initiation:</strong> Reduce the gap between knowing and doing.</li>
      <li><strong>Organisation:</strong> Create systems that reduce clutter, lost information, and confusion.</li>
      <li><strong>Working memory load:</strong> Get important details out of your head and into a usable system.</li>
      <li><strong>Follow-through:</strong> Improve completion so tasks do not remain half-started or mentally open.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="coachning-section" id="where-it-shows-up">
  <div class="coachning-panel">
    <h2>Where executive function difficulties often show up</h2>
    <p>
      The pattern is not always obvious at first. Some people look productive on the surface and still feel that ordinary life takes far too much effort. Others feel stuck, overloaded, or inconsistent in a way they cannot easily explain. Coaching helps identify where the breakdown actually happens.
    </p>
    <ul class="coachning-list">
      <li><strong>At work:</strong> prioritising, switching tasks, preparing for meetings, staying on top of admin, and finishing important work</li>
      <li><strong>In education:</strong> assignment planning, revision structure, deadlines, and independent study routines</li>
      <li><strong>At home:</strong> bills, appointments, routines, paperwork, household organisation, and remembering what is pending</li>
      <li><strong>Emotionally:</strong> shame, overwhelm, avoidance, and the stress that builds when too many things stay unfinished</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="coachning-section" id="how-coaching-works">
  <div class="coachning-panel">
    <h2>How executive function coaching works</h2>
    <p>
      Coaching starts by mapping the points where daily life keeps breaking down. Then we build around the situations that matter most to you. The goal is not a perfect planner or an elaborate method. The goal is a system that is light enough to use and strong enough to hold when life gets busy.
    </p>
    <div class="program-modules">
      <h4>Typical areas of work in coaching</h4>
      <ol class="module-list">
        <li>
          <span class="module-number">1</span>
          <div>
            <h5>Identify the friction points</h5>
            <p>See exactly where planning, starting, switching, or finishing keeps falling apart.</p>
          </div>
        </li>
        <li>
          <span class="module-number">2</span>
          <div>
            <h5>Build an external system</h5>
            <p>Create simple tools for priorities, reminders, sequencing, and task visibility.</p>
          </div>
        </li>
        <li>
          <span class="module-number">3</span>
          <div>
            <h5>Reduce overload</h5>
            <p>Lower the amount you are carrying mentally so energy can go into action rather than tracking.</p>
          </div>
        </li>
        <li>
          <span class="module-number">4</span>
          <div>
            <h5>Make the system stick</h5>
            <p>Adjust the structure until it is realistic enough to keep using over time.</p>
          </div>
        </li>
      </ol>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

    <p>
      Executive function difficulties are common in ADHD, autism, burnout, stress, and other situations where planning and self-management become harder. Coaching does not need to start with a label. It can start with the practical problem in front of you and build from there.
    </p>
    <ul class="coachning-list">
      <li>Useful if you already know you have ADHD or autism</li>
      <li>Useful if you do not have a diagnosis but recognise the pattern</li>
      <li>Useful when work, study, or home life keeps becoming harder to manage than it should be</li>
      <li>Useful when you want practical support without turning everything into theory first</li>
    </ul>
