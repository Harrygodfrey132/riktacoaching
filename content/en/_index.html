ADHD support options

Academic Tutor vs ADHD Coach: what is the difference?

An academic tutor often helps with target-based performance in specific areas. An ADHD coach helps you build sustainable daily functioning: planning, concentration, routines, regulation, and follow-through across life.

Focus area Academic Tutor ADHD Coach
Weekly checkups Weekly check-ins on agreed targets, tasks, and short-term performance goals. Weekly checkups focused on routines, concentration, stress load, and what blocked progress this week.
Main goal Improve performance on defined outcomes (for example coursework, revision goals, project tasks, or skill targets). Improve daily functioning, consistency, confidence, and independence with ADHD-aware systems.
Support on concentration Supports focus during sessions using prompts, structure, and task guidance. Builds concentration plans you can repeat alone: start rituals, timing blocks, distraction controls, and reset tools.
Specialist advice Provides strategy advice tied to their teaching domain and immediate task outcomes. Specialist ADHD advice for executive function, procrastination, overwhelm, emotional regulation, and realistic planning.
Planning and organisation Usually plans around specific outputs and deadlines over shorter windows. Creates full planning systems across deadlines, sleep, meals, transitions, admin tasks, and work/study demands.
Accountability Checks progress against assigned tasks or agreed deliverables. Tracks implementation of routines, habits, and behavioural experiments week by week with clear adjustments.
Communication and advocacy Often limited to guidance linked to the tasks they supervise. Supports scripts and communication plans for teachers, managers, family, and support services.
Emotional regulation Usually outside the role. Includes practical tools for frustration, shutdown, rejection sensitivity, and recovery after difficult days.
Between-session support Often limited to the tutoring session. Structured between-session tasks, simple trackers, and practical feedback to keep momentum.
Long-term outcome Better subject performance in the short term. Durable life systems that improve follow-through at school, university, work, and home.

Problem to solution

What people struggle with and how coaching helps

Many people already know what they should do, but struggle to do it consistently. ADHD and autism coaching turns recurring daily problems into practical, repeatable systems.

Problem people face How ADHD/Autism coaching supports them
“I can’t start tasks even when they are important.” We build task-initiation routines: 5-minute start rules, clear first steps, body-doubling structure, and low-friction launch plans.
“I get distracted and lose focus quickly.” We design concentration systems: time blocks, distraction mapping, workspace resets, and recovery methods when focus drops.
“My routines collapse after a few days.” We create realistic routines with flexibility tiers (minimum, standard, ideal) so progress continues even on low-energy days.
“I miss deadlines and underestimate time.” We use time-visibility tools: reverse planning, deadline anchors, buffer blocks, and weekly planning checkpoints.
“I feel constantly overwhelmed.” We reduce cognitive load by externalising tasks, prioritising essentials, and using simple decision frameworks.
“Emotions take over and I shut down.” We practice emotional regulation plans: early-warning signals, reset scripts, and step-by-step recovery after overload.
“Transitions are hard and derail my day.” We install transition systems for morning starts, school/work shifts, and evening wind-down so days feel steadier.
“School/work feedback says I am inconsistent.” We build accountability loops with weekly check-ins, clear behavioural targets, and practical adaptation between sessions.
“Social situations and communication feel exhausting.” We develop communication scripts, boundary tools, and planning for high-demand social moments to reduce burnout.
“I know my challenges but don’t know what to do next.” We turn insight into an action plan with measurable goals, weekly priorities, and concrete next steps.
