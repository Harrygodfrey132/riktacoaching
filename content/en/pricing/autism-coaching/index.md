  <div class="container">
    <header class="autism-coaching-breakdown-section__header">
      <p class="eyebrow">How The Process Works</p>
      <h2>The 6-stage autism coaching model</h2>
      <p>Each stage is designed to reduce overload, improve predictability, and build routines that are realistic for your context.</p>
    </header>
    <div class="autism-coaching-timeline">
      <article>
        <span>1</span>
        <h3>Profile and context mapping</h3>
        <p>We identify stress triggers, sensory load points, communication demands, and routine friction in daily life.</p>
      </article>
      <article>
        <span>2</span>
        <h3>Priority setting</h3>
        <p>We choose a small number of high-impact targets: school transitions, work sustainability, self-care, or social strain.</p>
      </article>
      <article>
        <span>3</span>
        <h3>Environment and routine design</h3>
        <p>We adjust structure, pacing, and expectations so the week becomes more predictable and manageable.</p>
      </article>
      <article>
        <span>4</span>
        <h3>Communication strategies</h3>
        <p>We develop direct scripts for boundaries, requests, conflict prevention, and clearer day-to-day communication.</p>
      </article>
      <article>
        <span>5</span>
        <h3>Energy and sensory regulation</h3>
        <p>We build practical regulation plans for overload, shutdown risk, recovery windows, and environmental adaptation.</p>
      </article>
      <article>
        <span>6</span>
        <h3>Long-term maintenance</h3>
        <p>Once systems hold, we move to maintenance sessions to support consistency through life changes and new demands.</p>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
