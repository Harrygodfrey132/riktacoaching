<section class="who-section adhd-definition" id="what-is-autism">
  <div class="container">
    <div class="who-grid intro-grid">
      <div class="intro-copy">
        <h2>How can autism show up in women?</h2>
        <p>
          Autism affects how the brain processes social contexts, sensory input, and sometimes which interests you have. In
          women it often shows as masking, perfectionism, or constant energy budgeting to get through the day.
        </p>
        <p>
          In the assessment we map how you’ve functioned in relationships, school, and work across your life. We distinguish
          autism from ADHD, burnout, PTSD, or hormonal shifts to give an accurate conclusion.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
          “An accurate autism assessment looks beyond masking and gives direction based on your strengths and circumstances.”
        </blockquote>
      </div>
      <div class="intro-stats adhd-definition-card">
        <h3>During the assessment we look at:</h3>
        <ul>
          <li>Masking strategies, social anxiety, and how your energy changes after social time.</li>
          <li>Sensory sensitivity, sleep, hormonal variation, and body signals.</li>
          <li>Special interests, strengths, and how you organise daily life, relationships, and family.</li>
          <li>Co-occurring conditions such as ADHD, anxiety, depression, or burnout.</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">
          We use tools like RAADS-14, AQ, and 3Di with clinical interviews and partner/family input when you think it would help.
        </p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="who-section adhd-process adhd-process--timeline" id="process">
  <div class="container">
    <div class="section-intro">
      <span class="eyebrow">Autism assessment for adults</span>
      <h2>A thorough process that brings clarity</h2>
      <p class="muted">
        Our adult autism assessment is structured and evidence-based. We determine whether you meet criteria on the spectrum and
        what support you need. The team follows national guidelines and makes sure you feel safe and understood throughout.
      </p>
      <p class="muted">
        Many come to us after long-term struggles in social situations or daily life, sometimes after a loved one’s diagnosis
        or recognising earlier challenges in hindsight. Whatever your situation, the goal is for you to better understand your
        strengths and difficulties and get the right help forward.
      </p>
    </div>
    <div class="timeline">
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>1</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Screening call</h3>
          <p>
            We start with a 30-minute screening call where you share your concerns and why you’re seeking assessment. We review
            current difficulties and what you hope to gain, and we decide if a full neuropsychiatric assessment is warranted.
            If yes, we plan next steps right away — if not, we offer advice and referrals.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>2</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>In-depth interviews and forms</h3>
          <p>
            A licensed psychologist gathers a detailed history from childhood to now, focusing on social interaction,
            communication, routines, and sensory sensitivity. When possible we interview someone who knew you in childhood.
            You complete standardised self-ratings that map autistic traits and related symptoms.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>3</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Psychological testing and medical review</h3>
          <p>
            You complete psychological tests of cognitive and social functions. In parallel a specialist physician or
            psychiatrist conducts a medical and psychiatric review to rule out other causes and identify co-occurring
            conditions such as ADHD, depression, or anxiety.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>4</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Multidisciplinary case review</h3>
          <p>
            Psychologist and psychiatrist review all information together and assess whether autism criteria are met and if other
            diagnoses are present. The aim is an accurate, evidence-based diagnosis and a full understanding of your profile.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>5</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Feedback and follow-up</h3>
          <p>
            We schedule a feedback session to go through results and conclusions verbally, and you receive a written report. You
            get tailored recommendations for daily life, work, psychoeducation, treatment, or referrals. Even if no diagnosis is
            given, you receive clear feedback and guidance on next steps.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

        <summary>I relate but “function too well” — can I still be diagnosed?</summary>

          experience, not just what things look like externally.
        </p>

      <details>
        <summary>How do you account for hormones and cycles?</summary>
        <p>

          recommendations.
        </p>
      </details>
      <details>
        <summary>Can I get support at work or in studies after the assessment?</summary>
