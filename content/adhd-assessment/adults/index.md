<section class="who-section adhd-definition" id="what-is-adhd">
  <div class="container">
    <div class="who-grid intro-grid">
      <div class="intro-copy">
        <h2>What does ADHD mean in adult life?</h2>
        <p>
          ADHD is a neurodevelopmental condition that affects how the brain regulates attention, motivation, and impulses.
          In adults it often shows up as inner restlessness, energy spikes followed by fatigue, or difficulty juggling several
          tracks at once.
        </p>
        <p>
          During the assessment we focus on how symptoms impact your work, relationships, and self-image. We also separate ADHD
          from other conditions that can look similar.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
          “A correct diagnosis should be meaningful for you and help with the difficulties you face. It gives you language and
          a way to build on your strengths.”
        </blockquote>
      </div>
      <div class="intro-stats adhd-definition-card">
        <h3>During the assessment we review:</h3>
        <ul>
          <li>Executive functions — your ability to plan and prioritise.</li>
          <li>Emotion regulation, impulsivity, and stress levels over time.</li>
          <li>Developmental history, masking strategies, and current supports.</li>
          <li>Medical background and co-occurring conditions that shape the picture.</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">
          We use tools like DIVA-5, validated forms, and welcome input from a partner or relative if you prefer.
        </p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="who-section adhd-process adhd-process--timeline" id="process">
  <div class="container">
    <div class="section-intro">
      <span class="eyebrow">Adult ADHD assessment</span>
      <h2>A process that brings clarity and the right support</h2>
      <p class="muted">
        Our adult ADHD assessment is designed to identify ADHD or related conditions in people 18 or older. Many seek help
        after long-standing difficulties with attention, organisation, restlessness, or impulsivity affecting work, studies,
        and relationships. The goal is clarity and the right treatment — for many it is a relief to understand the roots of
        their challenges.
      </p>
    </div>
    <div class="timeline">
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>1</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Screening call</h3>
          <p>
            We start with a 30-minute screening call. You share your concerns and why you want an assessment. We review your
            current difficulties and what you hope to gain. The call decides if a full neuropsychiatric assessment is needed.
            If yes, we plan next steps right away — if no, we offer advice and referrals.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>2</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>In-depth interviews and forms</h3>
          <p>
            A licensed psychologist gathers a detailed history from childhood to now — upbringing, school, work, relationships,
            and daily functioning — with focus on social interaction, attention, and hyperactivity. When possible we also
            interview someone who knew you in childhood. You complete standardised self-ratings that map ADHD traits and
            related symptoms.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>3</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Psychological testing and medical review</h3>
          <p>
            You complete psychological tests of cognitive and social functions. In parallel, a specialist doctor or
            psychiatrist performs a medical and psychiatric review to rule out other causes and identify co-occurring
            conditions such as autism, depression, or anxiety.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>4</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Multidisciplinary case review</h3>
          <p>
            Psychologist and psychiatrist review all information together and assess whether ADHD criteria are met and if other
            diagnoses are present. The aim is an accurate, evidence-based diagnosis.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>5</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Feedback and follow-up</h3>
          <p>
            We book a feedback session to go through results and conclusions verbally, and you receive a written report. You
            get tailored recommendations such as adjustments at home or work, psychoeducation, treatment, or referrals. Even
            without a diagnosis you receive clear feedback and guidance on next steps.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

      <h2>Frequently asked questions</h2>
      <p class="muted">Can’t find the answer? Reach out and we’ll guide you.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="adhd-faq-accordion">
      <details>
        <summary>Can I get an assessment via the public healthcare system?</summary>
        <p>
          Yes, many regions offer neuropsychiatric assessments via referral. We help you understand rights around self-referral
          and how our report can be used when speaking with public services.
        </p>
      </details>
      <details>
        <summary>What if I was never assessed as a child?</summary>
        <p>
          It’s common to discover ADHD in adulthood. We map childhood symptoms through interviews, records, and stories from
          people you choose to involve.
        </p>
      </details>
      <details>
        <summary>Is medication the only treatment?</summary>
        <p>
          No. We work with several tools: medication and/or coaching and therapy when appropriate.
        </p>
      </details>
      <details>
        <summary>What happens after I receive my report?</summary>
        <p>
          We offer follow-ups for medication, coaching, or therapy and can help with letters and coordination with your GP.
        </p>
      </details>
