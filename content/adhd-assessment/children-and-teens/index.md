<section class="who-section adhd-definition" id="what-is-adhd">
  <div class="container">
    <div class="who-grid intro-grid">
      <div class="intro-copy">
        <h2>What does ADHD mean for kids and teens?</h2>
        <p>
          ADHD affects how the brain regulates attention, impulses, and activity. In children it can show as uneven energy at
          school, strong emotions, or difficulty following instructions even when they want to succeed.
        </p>
        <p>
          In the assessment we look at how symptoms appear across settings — home, school, and with friends — and how strengths
          and challenges have developed over time. We focus on the whole picture: your child’s experience, family routines, and
          supports already in place.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
          “An assessment is about seeing the whole child. We want to give you the language and tools so both your child and you
          as caregivers feel secure moving forward.”
        </blockquote>
      </div>
      <div class="intro-stats adhd-definition-card">
        <h3>During the assessment we review:</h3>
        <ul>
          <li>Developmental history, strengths, and early signs of attention difficulties.</li>
          <li>School environment, homework, and how your child handles demands in different subjects.</li>
          <li>Social interaction, emotion regulation, and free-time interests.</li>
          <li>Family support, strategies, and any co-occurring conditions.</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">
          We use tools like K-SADS, 5-15, Conners, and teacher forms. We may add observations or conversations with teachers by
          agreement.
        </p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="who-section adhd-process adhd-process--timeline" id="process">
  <div class="container">
    <div class="section-intro">
      <span class="eyebrow">ADHD assessment for children</span>
      <h2>A careful process that creates clarity</h2>
      <p class="muted">
        Assessing ADHD in children is a structured process that highlights behaviors across settings. Parents often seek help
        for school challenges, peer issues, or home struggles with focus, activity level, and impulse control. The assessment
        brings clarity and opens the door to the right school support and everyday strategies.
      </p>
    </div>
    <div class="timeline">
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>1</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Initial parent consultation</h3>
          <p>
            We start with a 30-minute screening call to review developmental history and current concerns. After the call we
            decide if a full assessment is appropriate and outline next steps. If not, we give other recommendations. Depending
            on age, your child can join parts of the call.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>2</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Parent interviews and school input</h3>
          <p>
            An in-depth interview with you about your child’s development, strengths, and difficulties. You complete structured
            forms, and we gather material from school or preschool via teacher forms and sometimes a call to understand how your
            child functions in groups and learning environments.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>3</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Child observation and testing</h3>
          <p>
            A psychologist meets your child for play-based observation and age-appropriate tests of attention, social
            interaction, and cognition. We create a calm setting and pause as needed so your child feels safe.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>4</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Medical review</h3>
          <p>
            A child and adolescent psychiatrist or pediatrician conducts a medical assessment to rule out other causes and map
            co-occurring conditions.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>5</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Multidisciplinary case review</h3>
          <p>
            Psychologist and psychiatrist review all information together and assess whether ADHD criteria are met and if other
            diagnoses are present. The goal is an accurate, evidence-based diagnosis.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-node">
          <span>6</span>
        </div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h3>Feedback and support</h3>
          <p>
            We hold a feedback session to review results and conclusions verbally and provide a written report. You receive
            tailored recommendations for school support, any medical steps, and practical tools for home. When needed we refer
            to habilitation, speech therapy, occupational therapy, or psychological treatment.
          </p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

      <p class="muted">Can’t find the answer? Reach out and we’ll guide you.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="adhd-faq-accordion">
      <details>
        <summary>Does the school need to be involved?</summary>
        <p>
          We recommend at least one teacher completes forms and that we review any action plans. This makes the assessment more
          accurate and helps us propose the right accommodations.
        </p>
      </details>
      <details>
        <summary>Can we do the assessment if our child already sees CAMHS?</summary>
        <p>
          Yes. We’re happy to coordinate with existing care and ensure our report can be used in ongoing treatment or support
          applications.
        </p>
      </details>
      <details>
        <summary>Can the assessment be done online?</summary>
        <p>
          We can do most of the assessment digitally, and we can plan in-person visits when needed.
        </p>
      </details>
      <details>
        <summary>What if the assessment shows something other than ADHD?</summary>
        <p>
          We clearly describe our findings and guide you to the right next steps, whether that’s another neurodevelopmental
          condition, learning differences, or psychological support.
        </p>
      </details>
