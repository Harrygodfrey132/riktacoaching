What is ADHD?

Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder is a neurodevelopmental condition that affects how the brain regulates attention, motivation, and impulse control. It shows up differently for everyone—sometimes as restlessness, sometimes as burnout from masking symptoms, and sometimes as creative bursts that are hard to organise.

Our role is to make sense of those patterns with you. We explore how ADHD might influence your mood, sleep, executive functioning, and relationships, and we look for the strengths you already rely on. Where language or previous assessments felt confusing, we translate the science into practical insights you can use.

“ADHD isn’t a flaw—it’s a different way of processing information and energy. Understanding it can transform how you live and work.”

During your assessment we explore:

  • Concentration, planning, and time management in work, study, and home life.
  • Emotional regulation, overwhelm, and any co-occurring anxiety or mood concerns.
  • Developmental history and how symptoms evolved over time.
  • Protective factors, coping strategies, and support networks that already work for you.

We welcome input from partners, parents, or teachers when helpful and always with your consent.

What to expect

Your ADHD assessment journey

Each stage is paced around you. We balance clinical structure with space for questions so you leave with clarity, not more uncertainty.

1

Welcome & preparation

After booking you’ll receive a welcome pack with questionnaires, guidance for gathering school or workplace reports, and tips on how to prepare if you’re feeling nervous. We outline timelines and confirm who will attend each appointment.

  • Online forms designed to take 20–30 minutes.
  • Optional briefing call for parents or partners.
2

Clinical interview

A structured conversation covering strengths, daily routines, challenges, and medical history. We explore ADHD symptoms alongside any overlapping conditions such as anxiety, OCD, or autism, and we discuss what support has or hasn’t helped so far.

  • Conducted via secure video or at our clinics.
  • Space to share lived experience without judgement.
3

Diagnostic formulation

Your clinician analyses interview notes, rating scales, and collateral information to build a holistic profile. We look at how symptoms present across contexts and assess for differential diagnoses before confirming whether ADHD criteria are met.

  • Validated tools endorsed by NICE.
  • Clinical team case discussion when presentations are complex.
4

Feedback & next steps

We meet to walk through the results in plain language. You receive a detailed report with tailored recommendations, letters for work or education settings if needed, and a roadmap for ongoing support.

  • Practical strategies for focus, organisation, and wellbeing.
  • Medication planning and shared-care guidance when appropriate.

NICE

Our standards of care

We follow the National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE) guidelines to make sure every assessment is ethical, evidence-based, and collaborative. That means:

  • Using validated diagnostic tools across interviews, rating scales, and observational data.
  • Considering differential diagnoses, physical health factors, and safeguarding needs.
  • Working as a multi-disciplinary team so complex presentations receive shared input.

When medication is indicated we create shared-care plans with your GP and offer follow-up titration reviews.

Removing hurdles

Overcoming barriers to diagnosis

Reaching out for an assessment can feel daunting. We work to reduce the practical, financial, and emotional barriers that hold people back.

Awareness

ADHD presents differently in adults, women, and people of colour. We educate around less obvious signs—such as masking, rejection sensitivity, or burnout—so you feel seen rather than stereotyped.

Access to specialists

Flexible scheduling includes daytime, evening, and weekend slots. Choose a fully remote pathway or visit one of our partner clinics for in-person sessions when that feels more supportive.

Stigma

Many clients arrive after negative healthcare experiences. We provide trauma-informed care, clear explanations, and written summaries so you can advocate for yourself with confidence.

Cost

Installment plans and pay-per-session options help you spread the investment. We also signpost NHS right-to-choose pathways and employer funding routes where available.

FAQ

Answers to common questions

If you need detail not covered here, our team is happy to help—just contact us.

How long does an assessment take?

Adults typically book two 60–90 minute appointments spaced one to two weeks apart. Child assessments often include a third meeting with caregivers or teachers to ensure we capture different settings.

Do I need a GP referral?

You can self-refer. If you have a GP referral or previous reports, bring them—we review all relevant history and keep your GP informed with your consent.

Is it available online?

Yes. Secure video assessments follow the same NICE-guided pathway as in-clinic appointments. We’ll let you know if an in-person observation would add value and help arrange it.

What’s included in the report?

A clear diagnostic statement, summary of evidence, recommendations for reasonable adjustments, medication or therapy signposting, and template letters for work or education settings.

Is it confidential?

Absolutely. Your data is stored securely in line with UK GDPR. We only share information with third parties when you give written permission or when safety legislation requires it.

What happens after the assessment?

You can continue with follow-up psychiatry appointments, ADHD coaching, or group workshops. We can also liaise with your GP to support shared-care medication agreements.
