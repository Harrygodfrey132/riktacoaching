Understanding autism together

Autism is a lifelong neurotype that influences how people communicate, process information, sense the world, and connect with others. No two profiles are alike: some clients describe sensory intensity, others highlight deep focus, pattern recognition, or social exhaustion from masking.

Our clinicians take time to understand your lived experience, strengths, and the barriers you face in work, education, or relationships. We translate terminology, unpack previous reports where needed, and build a shared picture of what would make life feel more sustainable.

“An autism assessment should feel like being seen—validating the adaptations you already use while opening doors to new support.”

During your assessment we explore:

  • Sensory preferences, regulation strategies, and environments that help or overwhelm.
  • Communication styles, social energy, masking, and burnout.
  • Developmental history, interests, and routines that matter to you.
  • Co-occurring needs such as ADHD, anxiety, OCD, dyslexia, or dyspraxia.

We welcome trusted supporters when helpful and always centre your consent and comfort.

What to expect

Your autism assessment journey

Assessments blend structured diagnostic tools with reflective conversations. We move at your pace and outline every step in advance so there are no surprises.

1

Orientation & preparation

After booking you receive sensory-considerate guidance, optional questionnaires, and space to flag access needs. We confirm who will be involved and coordinate any school or partner input with your consent.

  • Preparation pack tailored for written or verbal preferences.
  • Optional pre-assessment call to reduce uncertainty.
2

Clinical conversations

Structured yet flexible interviews explore communication, sensory experiences, executive functioning, and wellbeing. We discuss contexts where you thrive and where sustained effort or masking leads to fatigue.

  • Remote or in-person sessions arranged around your preferred environment.
  • Support for sharing examples from work, study, or home life.
3

Collaborative formulation

Clinicians map your profile across diagnostic criteria, highlight strengths, consider differentials such as ADHD or trauma, and invite your reflections before finalising conclusions.

  • Evidence-based tools including ADOS-2 style observations when indicated.
  • Team input for complex, intersectional presentations.
4

Feedback & next steps

We meet to share findings in plain language, provide a detailed report, and map actionable adjustments for education, employment, or daily life. You choose what to share and with whom.

  • Practical recommendations anchored to your priorities.
  • Letters for workplaces, universities, or EHCP requests when needed.

NICE

Our standards of care

Assessments follow National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE) guidance alongside best practice from the British Psychological Society and Royal College of Psychiatrists.

  • Using validated diagnostic tools and multidisciplinary review where presentations are nuanced.
  • Assessing co-occurring conditions and safeguarding considerations with trauma-informed practice.
  • Providing clear, collaborative reports that you control and can share on your own terms.

We keep your GP, school, or employer informed only when you ask us to or when safeguarding legislation requires.

FAQ

Answers to common questions

If something is not covered here, please contact us and we will talk it through.

How long does an autism assessment take?

Adults usually complete two longer appointments across two to three weeks. Child pathways include additional sessions with caregivers or school staff to build a broader picture.

Do I need to prepare anything?

Bring examples that feel relevant—school reports, workplace adjustments, creative projects, or notes about sensory experiences. We provide prompts in advance so you can choose what to share.

Can everything happen online?

Yes. Secure video appointments follow the same NICE-guided structure. If an in-person observation would add value we will talk it through and arrange it with you.

Will the report help with adjustments?

Your report includes diagnostic conclusions, detail on strengths and support needs, and recommendations for reasonable adjustments. We can provide template letters for employers, universities, or Access to Work requests.

Is the process confidential?

Absolutely. Your information is stored securely under UK GDPR. We only share details with others when you give written consent or when safeguarding law requires it.

What support is available afterwards?

You can continue with follow-up psychiatry, occupational therapy, or coaching. We also connect you with peer communities and local services that align with your interests.
