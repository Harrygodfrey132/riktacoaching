  <section class="guide-section" id="what-is-adhd">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Basics</span>
        <h2>What is ADHD?</h2>
        <p>
          ADHD (Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder) is not about willpower or intelligence – it is about how the brain
          prioritizes information and regulates energy. Challenges with focus, sense of time, and impulse control affect
          planning, finishing tasks, and recovery.
        </p>
        <p>
          Descriptions similar to ADHD have existed since the 1700s. Today we know it is lifelong and can include hyperfocus,
          creativity, and persistence – but also overload when the environment demands something different from how the brain works.
        </p>
      </div>
      <div>
        <div class="guide-card-grid">
          <article class="guide-card guide-card--accent">
            <h3>Core difficulties</h3>
            <ul class="guide-card-list">
              <li>Controlling when and how long you can focus.</li>
              <li>Regulating energy and activity.</li>
              <li>Creating a pause between thought and action.</li>
            </ul>
          </article>
          <article class="guide-card">
            <h3>When it shows up</h3>
            <p>When demands for structure rise (school, work, family) or when earlier strategies are no longer enough.</p>
          </article>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="guide-section" id="how-common">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Prevalence</span>
        <h2>How common is ADHD?</h2>
        <p>ADHD exists across ages, genders, and groups. Numbers vary depending on access to assessment and how criteria are applied.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="guide-card-grid">
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Globally</h3>
          <ul class="guide-card-list">
            <li>Children &amp; teens: 5–7%.</li>
            <li>Adults: 2–5% (many remain undiagnosed).</li>
          </ul>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>In Sweden</h3>
          <ul class="guide-card-list">
            <li>High proportion of boys identified in school age.</li>
            <li>More women and adults receive diagnoses as awareness grows.</li>
          </ul>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>What rising numbers mean</h3>
          <p>Mainly better recognition and less stigma – not a biological increase.</p>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="guide-section" id="why-more-diagnoses">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Trends</span>
        <h2>Why are diagnoses increasing?</h2>
        <p>The change is driven by knowledge and access rather than “overdiagnosis.”</p>
      </div>
      <div class="guide-card-grid">
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Better criteria</h3>
          <p>Adult diagnostics have improved and capture more profiles than before.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Less underdiagnosis</h3>
          <p>Especially for girls and women who previously masked symptoms.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>More openness</h3>
          <p>Mental health is discussed more – more people seek help and assessment.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>The challenge</h3>
          <p>Higher volumes require quality-assured diagnostics so each person gets the right support.</p>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="guide-section" id="core-symptoms">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Symptoms</span>
        <h2>Core symptoms in ADHD</h2>
        <p>Everyone can relate sometimes – but in ADHD the difficulties are persistent and affect many areas of life.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="guide-card-grid">
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Inattention</h3>
          <p>Focus drops with monotonous tasks, details are missed, instructions forgotten, yet hyperfocus on interests is common.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Hyperactivity</h3>
          <p>Children move and talk a lot; adults often feel inner restlessness and a need to be busy.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Impulsivity</h3>
          <p>Quick decisions, interrupting others, difficulty waiting or weighing consequences.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Executive function &amp; time</h3>
          <p>Initiation, planning, and “time blindness” are common challenges.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Emotion regulation</h3>
          <p>Rapid mood shifts, sensitivity to stress or rejection.</p>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="guide-section" id="adhd-types">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Profiles</span>
        <h2>Types of ADHD (DSM-5)</h2>
        <p>Presentation can shift over time depending on age, demands, and strategies.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="guide-card-grid">
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Predominantly inattentive</h3>
          <p>Previously called ADD. More mental fatigue, distraction, and things “slipping away.”</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Hyperactive/impulsive</h3>
          <p>Shows as motor restlessness, impulsive communication, strong need for movement.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Combined</h3>
          <p>Most common in adults. Both attention and impulse control are affected.</p>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="guide-section" id="adhd-by-age">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Life span</span>
        <h2>ADHD at different ages</h2>
        <p>Symptoms change as demands change.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="guide-card-grid">
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Children</h3>
          <p>Attention issues show when demands for sitting still increase. Can be misread as defiance or “bad parenting.”</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Teens</h3>
          <p>More schoolwork and social cues raise the load; time management and organization become tougher.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Adults</h3>
          <p>Work, relationships, and family life demand planning and sustained focus; masking can be exhausting.</p>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="guide-section" id="masking">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Masking</span>
        <h2>Masking and “flying under the radar”</h2>
        <p>Many learn to hide difficulties – working harder, rehearsing conversations, double-checking everything.</p>
        <p>This can delay diagnosis and create burnout. A thorough assessment looks at both strengths and the cost of keeping up.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="guide-card-grid">
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Common signs</h3>
          <ul class="guide-card-list">
            <li>Over-preparing to avoid mistakes.</li>
            <li>Feeling “on stage” socially.</li>
            <li>Crashing with fatigue after masking.</li>
          </ul>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Why it matters</h3>
          <p>Masking hides support needs. When demands rise, strategies can break – leading to stress or depression.</p>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="guide-section" id="co-occurring">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Common overlaps</span>
        <h2>Conditions that often co-occur</h2>
        <p>ADHD can come with other neurodevelopmental or mental health conditions.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="guide-card-grid">
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Autism</h3>
          <p>Social communication differences and sensory sensitivities can overlap with ADHD challenges.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Anxiety or depression</h3>
          <p>Can arise from long-term stress, underperformance, or masking.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Sleep issues</h3>
          <p>Delayed sleep phase and restless nights are common and worsen focus.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Learning differences</h3>
          <p>Dyslexia, dyscalculia, or dyspraxia may need tailored strategies alongside ADHD support.</p>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="guide-section" id="gender">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Women &amp; ADHD</span>
        <h2>ADHD in women</h2>
        <p>Symptoms can look different – more inattention, mental overload, and emotion swings than overt hyperactivity.</p>
        <p>Many women are diagnosed later after years of compensating. Hormonal shifts (cycle, pregnancy, menopause) can change symptom intensity.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="guide-card-grid">
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Under-recognition</h3>
          <p>Quiet distraction or perfectionism can hide ADHD. Screening should include school history, masking, and burnout.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Care considerations</h3>
          <p>Medication and therapy plans may need to consider hormones, sleep, and co-occurring anxiety.</p>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="guide-section" id="causes">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Causes</span>
        <h2>What causes ADHD?</h2>
        <p>ADHD is strongly genetic. Brain networks for attention and regulation develop differently. Environment can affect how symptoms show up, but does not “cause” ADHD.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="guide-card-grid">
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Genetics</h3>
          <p>Runs in families; multiple genes involved.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Brain differences</h3>
          <p>Networks for reward, attention, and executive function activate differently and mature later.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Environment</h3>
          <p>Stress, sleep, and structure influence functioning and coping, but are not root causes.</p>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="guide-section" id="assessment">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Assessment</span>
        <h2>How an ADHD assessment works</h2>
        <p>A thorough assessment combines interviews, rating scales, and sometimes cognitive tests to map symptoms across life.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="guide-card-grid">
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>History &amp; interviews</h3>
          <p>Childhood and current symptoms, school/work history, and strengths.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Rating scales</h3>
          <p>From you and sometimes relatives/teachers to cross-check symptoms in different settings.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Medical review</h3>
          <p>Rule out other causes and check for co-occurring conditions.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Feedback</h3>
          <p>Clear results, written report, and a plan for support or treatment.</p>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="guide-section" id="treatment">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Treatment</span>
        <h2>Treatment and support</h2>
        <p>Effective care often combines medication, practical strategies, coaching, and adjustments at work or school.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="guide-card-grid">
        <article class="guide-card guide-card--accent">
          <h3>Medication</h3>
          <p>Stimulant or non-stimulant medication can improve attention and impulse control. Dosing is individualized and monitored.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Coaching &amp; therapy</h3>
          <p>Helps with planning, procrastination, emotion regulation, and communication.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Accommodations</h3>
          <p>Clear priorities, visual plans, noise control, flexible deadlines, and supportive tech.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Lifestyle</h3>
          <p>Sleep, movement, and nutrition support energy and focus.</p>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="guide-section" id="when-to-seek-help">
    <div class="container guide-section-grid">
      <div>
        <span class="guide-section-label">Next steps</span>
        <h2>When should you seek help?</h2>
        <p>If ADHD-like difficulties affect studies, work, relationships, or wellbeing, an assessment can clarify what is ADHD and what else might be involved.</p>
        <p>Early support reduces stress and prevents secondary problems like anxiety or depression.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="guide-card-grid">
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>Typical signals</h3>
          <ul class="guide-card-list">
            <li>Chronic procrastination and missed deadlines.</li>
            <li>Time blindness and disorganization despite effort.</li>
            <li>Emotional overload or frequent burnout.</li>
          </ul>
        </article>
        <article class="guide-card">
          <h3>How we help</h3>
          <p>Specialist assessment, medication management, and coaching tailored to your goals.</p>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
